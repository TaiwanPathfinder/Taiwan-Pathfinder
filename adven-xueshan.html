Xueshan Trekking

a two-day, two-night adventure to Xue Mountain’s Main and East Peaks! As Taiwan’s second-highest peak, Xue Mountain offers stunning cirque landscapes, alpine scenery, and sweeping views. With expert guides and smooth logistics, it’s the perfect way to experience high-altitude hiking without the hassle.

Embark on an unforgettable journey to Main Peak and East Peak of Xue Mountain, a breathtaking two-day, two-night adventure in Taiwan’s stunning mountain landscapes. Xue Mountain, the second-highest peak in Taiwan, boasts panoramic views, diverse alpine flora, and a unique opportunity to witness the beauty of Taiwan's glacial cirques.

This adventure is perfect for those curious about high-altitude hiking. With experienced guides and well-organized logistics, you’ll enjoy an immersive, hassle-free outdoor experience.

Meet at Taipei Main Station or another pre-arranged location. Round-trip transportation to the trailhead is included.
  • - Professional guides proficient in both English and Chinese
  • - Transportation from and to Taipei
  • - Accommodation at Qiksitea Cabin and 369 Camp
  • - 3 meals (Day 1: breakfast, dinner; Day 2: breakfast)
  • - National Park permit and insurance
  • - Each participant is covered by Specific Activity Insurance: NT$2 million for accidental death and disability, NT$200,000 for injury medical expenses
  • - The guide-to-participant ratio is at least 1:4, and guides are trained in first aid and accident management.
  • - All safety equipment provided; participants must follow guide instructions at all times.
  • - A detailed equipment list for the trek will be provided upon successful registration. Please ensure you prepare all items thoroughly. If you have any questions, feel free to reach out to us.
  • - Please bring your personal medication as needed. We recommend maintaining a regular exercise routine before the hike to ensure good physical fitness.

The main meals include Day 1 dinner, breakfastand and Day 2 breakfast are provided for the trekking. Clients need to bring their own snacks and lunches. If you have any special dietary requests, allergies, or needs, let us know and we’ll be happy to accommodate you.

Accommodation is arranged at the Qika Cabin and 369 Campsite, with tents and sleeping bags provided.

Trip Cancellation or Rescheduling Due to Weather

  • - Force Majeure: If the trip cannot proceed due to events such as a land typhoon warning, earthquake, cold wave, heavy rain advisory (as defined by the Central Weather Administration), road closures, or restrictions imposed by management authorities, a refund will be issued after deducting a 5% administrative fee.
  • - Poor Weather Forecast: If adverse weather such as continuous rain or an incoming cold front may affect the trip quality, we will conduct a comprehensive assessment. The final decision will be announced no later than 3 days before departure. If we decide to cancel the trip, a refund will be issued after deducting a 5% administrative fee.

Cancellation Due to Personal Reasons

  • - Cancel 14 days before departure: Refund minus a 5% administrative fee.
  • - Cancel 8–13 days before departure: 20% of the booking fee will be deducted.
  • - Cancel 3–7 days before departure: 30% of the booking fee will be deducted.
  • - Cancel within 2 days of departure: No refund.

Minimum Participant Requirement

  • - If the minimum participant number is not reached and the trip is canceled, participants may choose to reschedule for free or receive a refund with a 5% administrative fee deducted.
